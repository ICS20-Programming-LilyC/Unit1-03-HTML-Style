Countries and their flowers

England

The rose is the national flower of England. The rose was chosen after the civil wars that took place in the years 1455-1485. It was between the royal house of Lancaster whose emblem was a red rose and the royal house of York whose emblem was a white rose. This was why the wars were named The Wars of the Roses, which then gave England their national flower.

Cuba

The Hedychium coronarium also known as the Butterfly Lily is the national flower of Cuba. It was given this name for its similarity to a flying white butterfly. This flower gained its popularity in Cuba during the country's fight for independence, many women would relay secret war messages inside of these flowers.

Venezuela

In Venezuela their national flower is called the Cattleya Mossiae. This flower was declared as their national flower in 1951 because, since the XIX century it was used to decorate the Feast of the Crosses. Which is a cultural-religious celebration in Venezuela, where the cross symbolizes Jesus's cross which is decorated with these flowers asking God to bless them with no catastrophes.

India

For India their offical flower is aquatic flower, called a Lotus. This flower has been commonly used in art and mythology of ancient India. It has been present in many ancient traditions, scriptures and even mythology of India.
